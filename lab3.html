Idea 1: Deep work is Important

Newport argues that deep work allows you to do two things critical to your performance in the information economy

  1. Learn and master new skills:

    2. Newport explains that techology practices become obsolete quickly in the information economy. In order to stay relevant over decades, you must continue to learn challenging new skills-which requires focus.

  2. Apply the skills to increase your output.

    3. Once you have learned a skill, you need to do something useful with it. Consider the simple rule: High-quality work produced.

Idea 2: Deep work is Difficult

Newport explains that deep work is difficult because our world bombards us with near-constant distractions.He outlines three major ways that modern workplaces detail workers ability to engage in deep work

  1. Open floor plans:

    2. Open floor plans were meant to increase collaboration. But Newport explains that they are a continuously distracting enviroment, where every conversation is heard, and one person can disrupt dozens of people.

  2. Instant communication:

    3. With instant-messaging tools like Stack and texting, people can interrupt your work on-demand. According to Newport, as a result of this, we stop deing deep thinkers and become human network routers.

  3. Social media:

    4. On social media platforms, conversations continue endlessly, 24/7.
